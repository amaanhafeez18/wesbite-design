Wildlife 

____________________

 

Due to Pakistan's unique geography, wildlife is categorized into two distinct groups, one residing in the mountainous north and one in the coastal south.


Many places near tourist hotspots contain taxidermied remains of these unique species!

Wild life in the south

____________________

The southern region of Pakistan boasts a diverse range of wildlife, with species adapted to the region's hot and arid climate. Reptiles such as the Indian cobra and the saw-scaled viper are commonly found in the area, along with several species of fish including the Sindh dolphin, Indus baril, and Indus snow trout

 

  • Sindh dolphin
  • Indian cobra
  • Russell's viper
  • Saw-scaled viper
  • Chinkara (Indian gazelle)
  • Houbara bustard
  • Grey francolin
